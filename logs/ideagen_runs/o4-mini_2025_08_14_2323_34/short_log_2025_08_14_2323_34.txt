No dynamic feed-forward or non-linear elements are required, and we are *not* asking for state generation but for a *network supermap*.

1) Protocol Overview  
 • **Resources**  
   – Three probabilistic Bell-pair sources:  
     • Source_AB entangles modes (A₁,B₁)  
     • Source_AC entangles modes (A₂,C₁)  
     • Source_AD entangles modes (A₃,D₁)

   – Three intermediate Bell-state measurements (BSMs) realized by two-photon Hong–Ou–Mandel (HOM) fusions at nodes B, C, D.  
   – Two passive multiports (a 1→3 splitter at A, and a 3→1 combiner at E) built from beam splitters.  
   – Six number-resolving detectors behind the three fusion beam splitters (two per node) herald the identity‐outcome of each BSM (no Pauli correction needed).  

 • **Step 1 (Sender A)**  
   The input qubit (T₀,T₁) and the three ancilla halves (A₁,A₂,A₃) are fed into a **balanced 4-mode interferometer** that coherently distributes the qubit excitation over the three Bell-pair channels.  In effect,  
     |ψ⟩_T ⊗ |000⟩_A  →  (|ψ⟩₁|00⟩_{23} + |ψ⟩₂|00⟩_{13} + |ψ⟩₃|00⟩_{12})/√3,  
   where “₁,₂,₃” labels which Bell pair will carry the teleportation.  

 • **Step 2 (Intermediate BSMs)**  
   At each node X∈{B,C,D}, the traveling half-photon meets the corresponding other half (B₁,B₂,B₃) on a 50:50 beam splitter.  Post-selecting on the two-click outcome that projects onto the |ϕ⁺⟩ Bell state (no correction) heralds a successful teleportation along that branch.  Because we only accept the cases where *all three* BSMs click in their “identity” outcome, no feed-forward corrections are ever needed.  

 • **Step 3 (Receiver E)**  
   The three leftover halves of the Bell pairs (B′₁,C′₁,D′₁) then enter a **3→1 multiport combiner** that coherently recombines the three possible teleported amplitudes into a *single dual-rail output* (E₀,E₁).  Conditioned on the six-fold herald (two detectors at B, C, D each), the input state |ψ⟩_T now appears at (E₀,E₁) *in a coherent superposition of having traveled through B, C, and D*.

 • **Resource-efficient scaling:** By replacing the 1→3 and 3→1 multiports with 1→N and N→1, the same idea generalizes to N-arm teleportation superpositions—a whole family of new experiments.

3) Feasibility in Pytheus
