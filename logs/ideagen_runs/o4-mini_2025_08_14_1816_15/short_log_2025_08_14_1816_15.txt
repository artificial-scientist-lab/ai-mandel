We propose a delayed-choice entanglement-swapping network that directly measures a three-time-ordered correlator 
                  C(t₁, t₂, t₃) = ⟨M_A(t₁)·Choice_B(t₂)·M_C(t₃)⟩  
by combining measurement-induced entanglement with time-ordered measurements.

1. Overview and Scientific Goal  
   • Use two independent SPDC (probabilistic photon-pair) sources S₁ and S₂.  
   • S₁ emits photons 1→A and 2→B; S₂ emits 3→B and 4→C.  
   • A measures photon 1 at time t₁ in basis M_A (e.g. ±X).  
   • At time t₂ (after t₁ but before t₃), B performs a *choice* measurement on photons 2&3:  
     – Option 1: full Bell-state measurement (BSM), inducing entanglement between 1&4.  
     – Option 2: separable (local) polarization measurement on 2 and 3 (no entanglement).  
     The choice can be implemented via fixed optical routing (e.g. a motorized wave-plate setting) but no active feedback during a run.  
   • Photon 4 travels (via a delay line) to C and is measured at time t₃ in basis M_C (matching M_A).  
   • By post-selecting on the herald of the chosen measurement at B, one collects joint outcomes {m_A, c_B, m_C} and computes the three-time correlator  
        C = Σ m_A·c_B·m_C   P(m_A, c_B, m_C | herald).  
   • This probes how a measurement-induced entangling operation at an intermediate time influences correlations across space and time—combining “time-ordered correlator” with “measurement-induced entanglement.”

2. Experimental Layout (to be optimized by Pytheus)  
   – Sources: Two probabilistic photon-pair sources (modes 1–2 and 3–4).  
   – Delay lines: introduce delays so that A’s detection at t₁ precedes B’s joint measurement at t₂, which in turn precedes C’s detection at t₃.  
   – B’s measurement network: a linear-optics interferometer (beam splitters, phase shifters) that can be configured into a Bell-state analyzer or two independent analyzers. Pytheus finds the minimal-resource optical circuit realizing both cases with heralding detectors.  
   – Detectors: single-photon, number-resolving if needed, at A, B, and C for heralding and outcome readout.

3. Why This Is Novel and Impactful  
   – Past Pytheus tasks focus on generating entangled states. We instead target the *dynamics* of correlation through a network and explicitly measure time-ordered multi-time correlators in a measurement-induced entanglement context.  
   – This bridges foundational questions about quantum causality and temporal correlations with modern quantum-network tools (entanglement swapping, delayed-choice).  
   – It can be generalized: change basis choices M_A/M_C, vary t₁,t₂,t₃ delays, add more intermediate nodes for richer time-ordered correlators, or even higher-order correlators.

   – No dynamic feedback is required during a run: the “choice” at B is just a pre-selected static configuration per measurement run.
