I propose a “Quantum‐Metric Classifier Network” for photonic qudits:  
Goal  
— Take as input a single unknown qudit (dimension D), and K reference qudit states {|ψₖ⟩}.  
— In one fixed linear‐optical network, perform K parallel SWAP‐test/entanglement‐swapping subroutines that compare the input to each reference, and then route the input qudit into exactly one of K output ports labeled by the index k for which the Fubini–Study distance d_FS(|input⟩,|ψₖ⟩) is minimal.  
— The discrete detector clicks {det₀,…,det_{K−1}} are the quantized response: a single “1” at port k indicates “closest to |ψₖ⟩,” effectively performing a minimum‐metric classifier.  

Why it’s interesting & generalizable  
— Connects two modern concepts: the quantum metric (Fubini–Study distance as a measure of distinguishability) and quantized response (the discrete output port label k).  
— Pushes entanglement‐swapping/SWAP‐test modules beyond binary decision (swap vs. no‐swap) into a multi‐way decision network.  
— Yields a new primitive for quantum machine learning and adaptive networks: one can change K, D, or the set {|ψₖ⟩} without redesigning the conceptual protocol—only Pytheus needs to rerun the optical‐network synthesis.  
— No state‐generation target: the network performs a transformation + projective measurement (a classifier), not the preparation of any single entangled state.  

General structure (visualized top‐down):  
— Sources:  
   • 1 single‐photon source encoding the unknown qudit in D time‐bins or spatial modes.  
   • K probabilistic photon‐pair sources, each generating an entangled ancilla pair prepared (externally) in the reference state |ψₖ⟩ on one half and a local qudit on the other.  
— Network:  
   For each k=0…K−1:  
     • A SWAP‐test interferometer between the unknown qudit and the ancilla qudit from source k (realized by beam splitters, phase shifters, two‐photon interference).  
     • Entanglement‐swapping measurement of the remaining ancilla partner.  
     • These two joint projections give a “test‐success” signature only if the overlap ⟨input|ψₖ⟩ is maximal.  
— Outputs:  
   • K heralding detectors (one per k), arranged so that exactly one clicks in each successful run.  
   • A final “routing” beam splitter network that channels the input qudit into the physical output port k corresponding to the clicked herald.
