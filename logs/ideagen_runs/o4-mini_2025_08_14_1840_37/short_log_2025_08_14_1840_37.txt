Instead of state‐generation, the goal is to build a static linear‐optical network that enacts the global SWAP operator across two copies of an N‐mode fermionic register via entanglement swapping.  The same architecture generalizes to any N, opening a family of experiments probing entanglement spectra of free fermion models.

1) Target transformation (abstract):  
   • Input: two identical registers of N photonic qubits (representing two copies of the free‐fermion ground state).  
   • Operation: enact the SWAP test circuit—i.e. conditionally swap all N pairs of qubits—and measure the ancilla outcome to project onto symmetric vs. antisymmetric subspace across the two copies.  
   • Output: detection events whose parity signature gives Tr(ρ_A^2), hence the second Rényi entropy of subsystem A in the quadratic fermionic model.  

2) Sketch of the network (for N=2; generalizes to any N):  
   • Single‐photon emitters or SPDC sources generate four data photons in modes A1,A2 and B1,B2 (two copies).

   • Linear‐optical interferometer:  
       – Perform two parallel entanglement‐swapping modules (one per mode pair): each module mixes Ai with Ci and Bi with Di on beam splitters, followed by joint Bell detections on {Ai,Ci} and {Bi,Di}.  
       – Successful heralded outcomes enact a partial SWAP on (A,B) registers.  
   • Final projective measurement on ancilla detectors indicates symmetric vs. antisymmetric outcome across the two copies (the SWAP‐test result).  
   • Data detectors on A and B record coincident patterns that map to the eigenvalues of the SWAP operator.
