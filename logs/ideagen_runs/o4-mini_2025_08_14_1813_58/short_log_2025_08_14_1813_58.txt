By tuning the “dimerization” (alternating strong/weak bonds realized via adjustable beam-splitter transmissivities), the resulting teleportation fidelity as a function of bond ratio exhibits a sharp change exactly at the topological phase transition of the SSH model. In other words, the teleportation fidelity maps out the entanglement‐spectrum gap closing, giving a direct optical signature of the SSH entanglement spectrum.  

Visualization of the general structure:  
• Single‐photon emitter at mode A (Alice) carries the input qubit.  
• A linear chain of N photon-pair sources creates adjacent entangled links between modes in an alternating pattern (“strong” bond via two‐photon interference, “weak” bond via reduced-transmission beam splitters).  
• At each intermediate site, a Bell-state analyzer (linear optics + two detectors) performs entanglement swapping, chaining the entanglement toward the next link.  
• Ancillary detectors at each swap station herald success; only heralded runs propagate to the next stage.  
• The final node B (Bob) recovers the teleported qubit. A fifth party (Charlie) collects all heralding signals.  

Key points:  
– The experiment realizes a 1D SSH chain of entanglement-swapping operations.  
– Teleportation fidelity between A and B depends on the bond-strength ratio and reveals the entanglement‐spectrum transition.  
– No state generation or tomography is required; the protocol implements a quantum channel (teleportation) and measures its fidelity.  
– All elements (pair sources, single emitters, linear interferometers, photon-number–resolving detectors, Bell analyzers, heralding) are within Pytheus’s scope.
