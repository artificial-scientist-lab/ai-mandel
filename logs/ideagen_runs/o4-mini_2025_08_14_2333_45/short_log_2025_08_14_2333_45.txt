Each branch is composed of alternating “strong” and “weak” beam‐splitter couplings between neighboring nanofiber modes, realizing a 1D topological SSH chain that meets at a single central defect node.

At that central node, three inner branch modes are mixed in a symmetric 3×3 multiport linear‐optics circuit followed by three number‐resolving detectors. A specific three‐click coincidence pattern implements a joint Bell‐swap measurement, heralding the projection of the three remaining photons—one on each outer edge detector—into a genuinely multipartite entangled state. Because the branches are in a nontrivial topological phase, the mid‐junction defect mode is protected against moderate disorder in coupling strengths. By measuring the fidelity of the heralded GHZ‐like entanglement as a function of on‐demand disorder in the “weak” and “strong” couplings, one can directly probe the resilience of quantum‐network protocols embedded in topological photonic infrastructures.
