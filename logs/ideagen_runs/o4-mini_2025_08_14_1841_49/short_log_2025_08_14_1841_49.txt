We design a single static linear-optics network that implements the cyclic permutation (A→B, B→C, C→A) on three unknown single-rail photonic qubits (modes 0,1,2) in a **heralded** fashion, consuming a two-mode squeezed-vacuum ancilla (modes 3,4) as a nonclassical thermodynamic work reservoir. A click of exactly one photon in each ancilla detector flags a successful cyclic-SWAP; the three system qubits (modes 0–2) collapse into the permuted order and remain available at out_nodes for subsequent network stages.

Herald condition:
A click pattern of one photon in mode 3 and one photon in mode 4 projects the input state
 |a⟩₀ |b⟩₁ |c⟩₂ → |b⟩₀ |c⟩₁ |a⟩₂ 
for all a,b,c∈{0,1}, realizing the cyclic-SWAP gate with success probability boosted by the squeezing.
