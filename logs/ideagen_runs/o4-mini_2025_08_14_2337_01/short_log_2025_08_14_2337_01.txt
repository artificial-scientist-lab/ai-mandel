We start with two independent 4-dimensional OAM Bell pairs: one photon each at A & B, and one at C & D.  Externally, each photon passes through a q-plate + mode sorter that maps OAM parity→path qubit:
  even ℓ ∈ {0,2} → logical |0⟩,  
  odd  ℓ ∈ {1,3} → logical |1⟩.
This yields two qubit Bell‐pair sources: A–B and C–D, each in ∣00⟩+∣11⟩ (parity‐encoded).

At the central node, a single linear-optics network (designed by Pytheus) performs two qubit‐Bell measurements simultaneously:
  – On the “even” parity channels of B & C, swapping entanglement to A & D in the even subspace.
  – On the “odd” parity channels of B & C, swapping entanglement to B & C (!), or equivalently routing odd‐subspace entanglement back to the “inner” link.
  
Conditioned on two ancilla heralding clicks, the outputs are:
  – A–D share the original even-ℓ Bell pair,
  – B–C share the original odd-ℓ Bell pair.
  
By multiplexing these two parity‐swaps into one ancilla-assisted network, we save resources compared to two separate 2-qubit swaps.  This construction generalizes to any d: split into subspaces by arbitrary mode‐sorters (not just parity), then multiplex many subspace-swaps in one shot.
