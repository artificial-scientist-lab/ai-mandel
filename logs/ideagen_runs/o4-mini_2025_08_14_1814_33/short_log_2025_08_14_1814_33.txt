Description  
We implement a two-node quantum router: a “control” qubit encoded in two spectral modes at Alice’s side (modes 0 and 1) coherently routes a second qubit (the “data” qubit) encoded in two spatial modes at the same site (modes 2 and 3) onto one of two remote sites, Charlie (modes 4,5) or David (modes 6,7), in perfect quantum coherence. In superposition, the router produces  
 |0⟩_spec⊗|ψ⟩_spatial → |0⟩_spec⊗|ψ⟩_Charlie  
 |1⟩_spec⊗|ψ⟩_spatial → |1⟩_spec⊗|ψ⟩_David  
and thus for an arbitrary input α|0⟩+β|1⟩ controlling an arbitrary spatial qubit |ψ⟩, outputs the entangled superposition α|0〉|ψ〉_C + β|1〉|ψ〉_D.  

Visualization  
Sources:  
 • One heralded single-photon (or pair) source prepares the control qubit in an arbitrary superposition of two spectral modes (0,1).  
 • A second source prepares the data qubit in arbitrary spatial superposition of modes (2,3).  

Setup (linear network):  
 • A small interferometer of beam splitters and phase shifters implements a controlled-SWAP: control input modes (0,1) are mixed via tunable phases with ancilla, such that the spatial modes (2,3) are routed to output ports (4,5) or (6,7) depending coherently on whether the photon occupies spectral mode 0 or 1.  
 • All intermediate ancilla modes are measured by number-resolving detectors; successful heralding events indicate the router succeeded.  

Detectors:  
 • Output detectors at Charlie’s modes [4,5] record the data qubit when the router control is “0.”  
 • Output detectors at David’s modes [6,7] record the data qubit when the router control is “1.”  
 • Ancilla detectors are used to post-select successful controlled-SWAP operations.  

Target transformation (abstract):  
 • Input nodes: spec-control = [0,1], data = [2,3]  
 • Ancilla detectors: [8,..,n]  
 • Output nodes: Charlie = [4,5], David = [6,7]
