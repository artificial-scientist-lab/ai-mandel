We propose a *Quantum‐Entanglement Router* built from two separate nanophotonic chips (Alice and Bob) linked via a hybrid plasmonic waveguide to a central nanophotonic routing node (Charlie). The goal is to *multiplex* entanglement on demand between Alice–Bob, Alice–Charlie or Bob–Charlie by performing different heralded linear‐optical measurements at the router.

Visualization of logical flow:
• Alice chip emits photons A1,A2 via a pair‐source  
• Bob chip emits photons B1,B2 via an independent pair‐source  
• A1 & B1 travel to intermediate plasmonic–nanophotonic interfaces into Charlie’s router  
• Router mixes A1,B1 plus two ancilla photons C1,C2 in a 4-mode linear network  
• Four detectors at Charlie herald one of three distinct routing outcomes:
    – Click pattern “00” → A2 entangled with B2  
    – Click pattern “01” → A2 entangled with C3  
    – Click pattern “10” → B2 entangled with C3  
• Depending on which pair is entangled, Alice, Bob or Charlie share instantaneous entanglement.

Key pytheus config ingredients:
• Two independent probabilistic pair‐sources (no direct connections between A and B, enforced by removed_connections).  
• Four ancilla input modes at the router, four detectors (Charlie’s heralding).  
• Linear-optical mesh at the router to realize a 4-mode unitary that, when followed by photon‐number‐resolving detection, implements three distinct Bell‐swap channels.  
• Target transformation expressed abstractly: three conditional two-qubit Bell states on modes {A2,B2}, {A2,C3}, {B2,C3} flagged by different detector outcomes.

Why novel?  
This is the first *multiplexed* entanglement‐routing network: instead of one fixed entanglement swap, the same hardware routes entanglement into different parties on demand by post-selection. It generalizes swapping to a small “quantum router” unit that can be cascaded for larger networks.

Why feasible?  
Pytheus can optimize:
– the 4-mode unitary at Charlie (linear-optical mesh)  
– the assignment of sources to modes subject to removed_connections  
– the heralding patterns that project onto different two-qubit Bell states  
All within its abstract‐state, probabilistic‐pair, linear-optics framework—no cavities or feedback required.
