Explanation:  
We have four spatial nodes A, B, C, D that wish to share entanglement between A and D.  Two entanglement‐swapping orders are possible: Swap₁ = B then C, Swap₂ = C then B.  We place these two orders in coherent superposition under a photonic control qubit |ctrl⟩ = α|0⟩+β|1⟩.  Depending on |ctrl⟩, either (i) Bell‐measure at B→C then C→D or (ii) C→B then B→D, teleports entanglement to A–D.  The final post-selected output is  
|Ψ_out⟩ = α|0⟩_ctrl ⊗ |Φ⁺⟩_{A,D}^{(order B→C)} + β|1⟩_ctrl ⊗ |Φ⁺⟩_{A,D}^{(order C→B)},  
where |Φ⁺⟩_{A,D}^{(…)} indicates the Bell state produced by the corresponding swap order.
