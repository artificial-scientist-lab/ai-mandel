Here’s a concrete target for Pytheus: a four‐party entanglement–swapping network that implements a semi-quantum key agreement (SQ-QKA) among two fully quantum nodes (Q₁, Q₂) and two semi-quantum nodes (C₁, C₂).  

1. Parties & capabilities  
  - Q₁ & Q₂ (quantum): can prepare Bell pairs, choose arbitrary single-photon measurements.  
  - C₁ & C₂ (semi-quantum): each can only (a) measure an incoming photon in the Z-basis or (b) reflect it undisturbed toward the central swapping station.  
  - S (swapper): a central node performing a Bell-state measurement on two incoming photons.  

2. Protocol outline  
  a) Q₁ emits a Bell pair (p₁→C₁, p₂→S). Q₂ emits another Bell pair (p₃→C₂, p₄→S).  
  b) C₁ decides reflect or measure:  
     - If measure: it performs a Z-basis detection on p₁, records bit b₁, and absorbs the photon.  
     - If reflect: it sends p₁ to S.  
     Similarly for C₂ on p₃.  
  c) At S, any two reflected photons undergo a Bell-measurement. A successful click pattern heralds entanglement or correlates the classical outcomes.  
  d) Q₁ & Q₂ measure their retained photons (p₂, p₄) in predetermined bases, combining results with b₁, b₂ to establish a shared key.  

3. Target for Pytheus  
  - Emitters: four probabilistic photon-pair sources for pairs (Q₁–C₁), (Q₂–C₂).  
  - Network: a linear-optical circuit that routes C₁/C₂ reflections into the Bell-analyzer and routes measure-outcomes to Z-detectors.  
  - Detectors:  
      • Two “measure” detectors at each Cᵢ realizing Z-basis  
      • Two detectors at Q₁, Q₂ for their Bell-pair halves  
      • Two (“ancilla”) detectors at S for the Bell-measurement  
  - Removed_connections: forbid any direct pair source from sending both photons to the same Qᵢ or Cᵢ (enforcing non-locality).  
  - Num_anc: minimal ancilla detectors at S for Bell-measurement (2).  

4. Why this matters  
  - It generalizes entanglement-swapping QKD to a key‐agreement setting where two classical‐limited parties participate.  
  - It’s a stepping stone to larger semi-quantum networks (rings, stars) for multi-party key agreement.  
  - Pytheus can optimize beam-splitter settings and interferometric layout to maximize heralding rate and key‐rate.  

This target invites exploration of resource trade-offs (number of sources, ancillas, success probability) in semi-quantum key agreement networks and paves the way for experimental and theoretical advances in hybrid classical–quantum cryptography.
