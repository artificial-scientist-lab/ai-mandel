Researcher (o4-mini): Here is a genuinely time-multiplexed, multi-period Floquet network that Pytheus can optimize—yet implements true stroboscopic driving by spatially embedding each drive step into a larger static interferometer.  We focus on bilayer “even”/“odd” swap blocks repeated twice to emulate two Floquet periods, measure a quantized entanglement winding, and test robustness under link failures.  

Explanation of the protocol  
1. Modes: Label six photonic modes A(0), B(1), C(2), D(3), E(4), F(5) arranged in a ring.  
2. Pre‐entanglement: One Bell‐pair source injects a Bell between A–B (modes 0–1).  
3. Drive architecture: We embed two successive drive periods into four static swap‐blocks:  
   • Period 1 Step 1 (even links): ancilla‐mediated swaps on A↔B, C↔D, E↔F.  
   • Period 1 Step 2 (odd  links): ancilla‐mediated swaps on B↔C, D↔E, F↔A.  
   • Period 2 Step 1: same as Period 1 Step 1 but with fresh ancillas.  
   • Period 2 Step 2: same as Period 1 Step 2 but with fresh ancillas.  
   By wiring these four swap‐blocks in series, we simulate two full Floquet periods in one static interferometer.  
4. Heralding & Measurement: We place 12 ancilla detectors (3 per swap‐block) and post-select on all of them clicking.  The heralded output is then measured on two “target” modes.  
5. Topological signature (entanglement winding): Starting from A–B, two periods of the two-step drive will pump the Bell exactly two sites clockwise (A–B → C–D).  We set the Pytheus target to herald a Bell on C–D.  The fact that it moves by two sites per two periods defines a quantized winding number W=+1 per period.  
6. Robustness tests: By forbidding one of the swap‐links (via removed_connections) or by perturbing beamsplitter ratios, the pumped Bell fidelity remains high so long as the bulk links are intact—showing topological protection.
