Description:
Implement a 2×2 “quantum percolation” network for semi-quantum key distribution.  
– Four corner nodes (A, B, C, D) are “semi-quantum” classical: they can only reflect incoming photons or, at the very end, measure in the Z-basis (simulated by placing number-resolving detectors after successful herald).  
– Four entangled(pair) sources sit on the edges AB, BC, CD, and DA of the square, each emitting one photon toward its two adjacent corners (e.g. source AB emits to A and B).  
– Two quantum servers (S₁ and S₂) sit at the midpoints of the two diagonals (between A–C and B–D). Each server performs a Bell-state measurement on the two incoming photons from its two adjacent edge-sources, using ancilla detectors.  
– When both Bell measurements succeed (heralded by clicks in the ancilla detectors at S₁ and S₂), A and C (resp. B and D) become entangled across the network’s “percolated” diagonal.  
– Finally, corner nodes A and C (or B and D) measure in Z-basis to extract correlated bits—the raw key.
