Description  
  • Graphs  
    – Start with N = 4 (generalizable to N > 4). Label the top row nodes 0,1,2,3 and bottom row 4,5,6,7.  
    – Edges (Bell‐pair sources) on each “rung” (0–4,1–5,2–6,3–7) and each “rail” (0–1,1–2,2–3 and 4–5,5–6,6–7).  
    – Cylinder boundary: connect top 3–0 and bottom 7–4 with two more pair sources.  
    – Möbius boundary: connect top 3–0 and bottom 7–4 but swap their rail‐connections (i.e. 3–0 as usual, but connect bottom 7 to top 1 and 4 to bottom 6), creating a half‐twist.  

  • Task  
    – Using only these pair sources and local linear-optical Bell measurements (entanglement swapping) at intermediate nodes {1,2,5,6}, generate heralded entanglement between node 0 and node 4.  
    – Optimize, via Pytheus, the placement of additional beam splitters, phase shifters, and ancillary detectors to maximize fidelity (or heralding probability) of the 0–4 Bell pair.  

  • Comparison  
    – Run one optimization for the cylinder graph and one for the Möbius graph.  
    – Quantify how the non-orientable twist raises or lowers the resource overhead (number of ancillas, success rate) compared to the orientable cylinder.
