3) Static routing multiport at A:
   • Surviving mode A₁′ and A₂′ enter a 50:50 BS network whose design ensures that only the “live” qubit (the one that suffered no-jump) emerges in a well-defined temporal/spatial mode A_r  
   • The “dead” qubit (jumped) is diverted to a dump port (no further use).  
   • No dynamic switching: the two-BS network plus ancilla heralding automatically postselects which branch is active.  

4) End-node tomography (tomographic entanglement indicators):
   • Node B and node C each have an identical fixed linear‐optical multiport (two-phase shifters + two beam splitters) connected to two detectors.  
   • These multiports implement the minimal three-setting joint measurement needed for a two-qubit entanglement witness W (e.g. the projector-sum witness built from XX, YY, and ZZ correlations).  
   • When “routed to B,” detectors at B yield a negative 〈W_B〉 (<0) certifying entanglement on A_r–B; at C, 〈W_C〉 remains ≥0. Vice versa when routed to C.

Mini-Abstract:  
We implement a fully static, loss-controlled quantum router that uses two SPDC Bell-pair sources and an amplitude-damping switch—heralded by ancilla photon clicks—to branch-selectively route entanglement from source A either to node B or C without dynamic feedback or forbidden elements. End-to-end tomography at the chosen node measures the three projector outcomes specified for the selected entanglement witness (the explicit target quantum set), thereby certifying entanglement only in the routed branch.
