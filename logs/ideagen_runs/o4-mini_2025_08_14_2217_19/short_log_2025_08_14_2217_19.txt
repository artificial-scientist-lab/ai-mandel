Researcher (o4-mini): Here is the proposed experiment—“Indefinite‐Order Quantum‐Repeater Primitive: Superposing Purification and Swapping via Near‐Field Scatterer”:

Explanation:  
In a standard two‐link repeater you have two noisy EPR pairs, A–X and X–B. To extend entanglement to A–B you either  
  • (i) Purify each link locally at X, then perform entanglement‐swapping on the purified pairs (Purify→Swap), or  
  • (ii) First swap the noisy pairs (Swap→Purify) and then purify the long noisy link.  
These two operations do not commute and yield different final fidelities. We now put “Purify” and “Swap” into quantum superposition, thereby creating a genuine indefinite‐causal‐order repeater primitive.  

Setup:  
• Pair source S₁ emits an EPR pair on modes (0:A, 1:X₁).  
• Pair source S₂ emits an EPR pair on modes (2:X₂, 3:B).  
• Ancilla pair S₃ emits an EPR pair on modes (4:A₃, 5:A₄).  
• Single‐photon ancilla emitter E_ctrl prepares |+⟩ on mode 6.  

All five photons at X (modes 1,2,4,5,6) enter a single 5×5 linear‐optics multiport (the “near‐field scatterer”), whose internal beam‐splitter ratios and phases are tuned so that:  
 – If the ancilla is in |0⟩, the device implements in sequence Purification on modes (1,2) (a parity‐check measurement using modes 4,5) followed by a Bell‐measurement on the purified pair;  
 – If the ancilla is in |1⟩, it instead implements first the Bell‐measurement (swap) on the noisy pair (1,2) and then the parity‐check purification on the swapped link, again using modes (4,5).  

Heralding detectors D₇…D₁₀ number‐resolve one photon each from the four measurement‐outputs.  A four‐click event {1,1,1,1} plus a final Hadamard‐measurement on the ancilla (mode 6) post‐selects the superposition  
   (|Purify→Swap⟩ + |Swap→Purify⟩)/√2  
between Alice’s mode 0 and Bob’s mode 3.  

Sources & Modes Summary:  
 • S₁ → [0:A,   1:X₁]  
 • S₂ → [2:X₂,  3:B ]  
 • S₃ → [4:A₃,  5:A₄]   (ancilla resources for both steps)  
 • E_ctrl →  6:control‐photon (|+⟩)  

Network Flow:

    ↘  
     ↘  
    Near‐Field Scatterer (modes 1,2,4,5,6)  
    ↗         ↖  
  [3] B   Heralding Detectors D₇…D₁₀
